SAPIRA — BRAND CONTEXT

------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT SAPIRA IS

Sapira is an operational intelligence company.

It builds infrastructure for how businesses actually run — not how they're supposed to run on paper.

Sapira captures the tacit coordination layer of an organization: the informal rules, the manual handoffs, the knowledge that lives in people's heads — and turns it into a system.

------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT PHARO IS

Pharo is Sapira's core product.

It is not software in the conventional sense.
It is infrastructure.

Pharo sits on top of existing enterprise systems — ERPs, spreadsheets, legacy tools — and automates the coordination work that today is done manually by people.

It does not replace existing systems.
It connects them, learns how the business operates, and eliminates the invisible friction between them.

Pharo is:
- a coordination engine
- a memory layer for operations
- a systemization of tacit knowledge

Pharo is NOT:
- a dashboard
- a reporting tool
- an AI chatbot
- a workflow builder
- a generic automation platform

------------------------------------------------------------

TARGET CUSTOMER

European mid-to-large enterprises:
- Revenue: €70M – €2B
- Sectors: manufacturing, financial services, logistics, retail, hospitality
- Profile: operationally complex, process-heavy, dependent on human coordination

Decision-makers:
- COOs, heads of operations
- Not CTOs, not IT departments

Pain: coordination overhead, lost context between systems, operational fragility when key people leave.

The ability to know how your business actually runs, and make that permanent.

MESSAGING PRINCIPLES

1. Control over convenience
   The product is about certainty and permanence, not speed or ease.

2. Infrastructure framing
   Always position Pharo as infrastructure — not tooling, not software.

3. Tacit knowledge as the real asset
   The insight is that most enterprise knowledge is not in any system.
   Sapira captures it and makes it permanent.

4. Human coordination is the problem
   Not bad software. Not missing data. The invisible work between systems.

5. European enterprise as context
   Implies rigor, compliance, operational complexity — not hypergrowth.

6. Restraint signals quality
   Saying less signals that we know more.
   Avoid the impulse to explain everything.
